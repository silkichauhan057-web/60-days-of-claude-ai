# Day 14 — Job Red Flag Detector

## Task
Used an "AI Red Flag Detector" prompt to analyze a Job Description and Company Information for unrealistic requirements, toxic workplace signals, remote job authenticity issues, hiring risks, and company risks.

## JD & Company Info Used
No real job posting was available, so a **synthetic, fictional example** was created for demonstration purposes — a mixed JD with both legitimate elements and deliberate red flags (contradictory experience requirements, hustle-culture language, hidden office requirement, undisclosed salary). Company name ("Nexalyte Solutions") and all details are made up and do not represent any real organization.

## Report Summary

### Overall Risk Score: 71/100 (High Risk)

### Top Red Flags
| Flag | Severity |
|---|---|
| Contradictory experience requirement (Entry Level but 3-5 yrs) | 9/10 |
| "Remote" role requires client office travel | 8/10 |
| Weekend/crunch-period work expected | 8/10 |
| "Expert-level" in 7 tools for an entry role | 7/10 |
| Scope creep across data/marketing/HR/client calls | 7/10 |
| No salary disclosed | 7/10 |
| "Rockstar" + "wear many hats" + "fast-paced" language | 6/10 |
| Founder track record includes a shut-down company | 6/10 |
| Minimal public company presence | 5/10 |

### Positive Signals
- Freshers explicitly welcomed despite stated experience range
- Core responsibilities (SQL, dashboards, data cleaning) are legitimate, real Data Analyst work
- Small team size could mean genuine hands-on exposure
- Perks stated plainly, not hidden behind vague language

### Risk Breakdown
| Category | Risk Level |
|---|---|
| Requirements | High |
| Culture | High |
| Remote | Medium-High |
| Hiring | Medium |
| Company | Medium |

### Final Verdict
⚠️ **Apply with Caution** — core work is real and relevant, but contradictions and hustle-culture signals warrant clarifying questions before accepting any offer.

### 5 Smart Interview Questions Generated
1. Clarify client office travel frequency for the "remote" role
2. Ask what a realistic candidate profile looks like given the experience contradiction
3. Ask for a walkthrough of a typical week across the listed responsibilities
4. Ask what "flexible hours during crunch periods" means in practice, and whether it's compensated
5. Ask directly for the compensation range and company funding/growth stage

## Key Learnings
- A JD can contain both real, legitimate responsibilities *and* red flags at the same time — the goal isn't a binary "good JD / bad JD" but a nuanced risk breakdown across categories.
- Contradictions (e.g., "Entry Level" + "3-5 years required") are often more revealing than any single red flag phrase — they signal the posting wasn't carefully written, which itself is a data point.
- "Wear many hats," "rockstar," and "fast-paced" are pattern-level signals, not automatic disqualifiers — small/early companies legitimately need versatile people; the concern is when this language stacks with undisclosed pay and undefined boundaries.
- Turning identified risks into specific interview questions is more useful than the risk list alone — it gives a concrete next action instead of just anxiety.
- For any red-flag analysis tool, disclosing when a synthetic/example input was used (vs. a real posting) matters — presenting a fictional company's risks as if they were real would be misleading.
